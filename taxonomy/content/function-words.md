# Function words content sheet v1 — teaching descriptions

Hand-authored fixes from the descriptive-clues audit (June 2026). These are the
abstract closed-class words where the generated descriptions broke the voice:
metalinguistic definitions ("a tiny word you put before a naming word"), idioms
("hard on the ears", "set the story straight"), circular definitions ("'if' is
a what-if"), and one outright wrong script ("never" → "I always keep trying").

The rule for every entry here: **never define a function word — show it working
in a familiar moment.** Meaning through a concrete routine → two everyday
examples → a phrase the child can actually deploy. No grammar words (sentence,
word, naming word), no idioms, opposites kept adjacent where they teach.

---

## Negation & refusal (the protest words — highest communicative value)

### not
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Not' is how you say something is wrong, or isn't what you want."
  2. "'Not this one' means a different one. 'Not yet' means wait a little."
  3. "You can say 'not this one' or 'I'm not done.'"

### don't
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Don't' is how you tell someone to stop, or not to do a thing."
  2. "'Don't touch' means hands off. 'Don't go' means stay with me."
  3. "You can say 'don't!' or 'please don't go.'"

### never
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Never' means it does not happen — not even one time."
  2. "We never touch a hot stove. Crayons never go in your mouth."
  3. "You can say 'never!' when you really, really don't want something."

## Little words before things

### a
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'A' means one — any one of something."
  2. "'A cookie' means one cookie. 'A dog' means one dog."
  3. "You can say 'a cookie, please' or 'I see a dog!'"

### the
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'The' means that exact one — the one you both know."
  2. "'The red cup' is your special red cup, not just any cup."
  3. "You can say 'open the door' or 'the big one!'"

### of
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Of' tells what is inside, or what something is a piece from."
  2. "'A cup of milk' is a cup with milk inside. 'A piece of cake' is one piece from the whole cake."
  3. "You can say 'a cup of milk' or 'a piece of cake.'"

### any
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Any' means even one little bit."
  2. "'Any more?' asks if there is still some left. 'Not any' means all gone."
  3. "You can say 'any more, please?' or 'I don't see any.'"

## Being & having (now vs. already finished)

### are
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Are' goes with 'you' and 'we' to say what is happening right now."
  2. "'We are playing' means the playing is happening right now."
  3. "You can say 'you are funny' or 'we are here!'"

### was
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Was' is for something that already happened — it is finished now."
  2. "'It was fun' means the fun already happened, before now."
  3. "You can say 'it was yummy' or 'I was sleeping.'"

### have
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Have' means it is yours, or it is in your hands right now."
  2. "'I have a ball' means the ball is yours to hold."
  3. "You can say 'I have it!' or 'Can I have one?'"

### has
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Has' means someone else is holding it, or it is theirs."
  2. "'She has the ball' means the ball is in her hands."
  3. "You can say 'he has it' or 'Mom has my cup.'"

### had
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Had' means you held it before, but that is finished now."
  2. "'I had a cookie' means the cookie is already gone."
  3. "You can say 'I had one' or 'we had fun.'"

## People words

### him
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Him' is the boy or man you are talking about."
  2. "'I see him' means you see that boy. 'Give it to him' means put it in his hands."
  3. "You can say 'I see him' or 'give it to him.'"

### her
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Her' is the girl or woman you are talking about."
  2. "'I see her' means you see that girl. 'Her cup' means the cup that is hers."
  3. "You can say 'I see her' or 'give it to her.'"

### hers
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Hers' means it belongs to her — it is her thing."
  2. "If the cup is hers, it is her cup, not yours."
  3. "You can say 'that one is hers.'"

## Joining & maybe words

### but
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'But' means here comes something different, or a problem."
  2. "I want to play, but it's bedtime. It's little, but it's strong."
  3. "You can say 'I want it, but it's gone.'"

### so
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'So' tells what happened next because of it."
  2. "I was tired, so I slept. It broke, so we fixed it."
  3. "You can say 'I was hungry, so I ate.'"

### if
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'If' means maybe — it only happens when another thing happens first."
  2. "If it rains, we stay inside. If you're hungry, we eat."
  3. "You can say 'if you want' or 'what if?'"

### could
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Could' is a kind, gentle way to ask for something."
  2. "'Could I have one?' asks nicely. 'Could you help?' asks for help kindly."
  3. "You can say 'could I have a turn?'"

### would
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Would' is a polite way to ask, or to say what you'd like."
  2. "'Would you help me?' asks kindly. 'I would like that' means yes, please."
  3. "You can say 'I would like more, please.'"

## Sound words (fix idioms)

### loudly
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Loudly' means with a big, strong sound."
  2. "A drum bangs loudly. A lion roars loudly."
  3. "You can say 'too loud!' or 'sing loudly!'"

### quietly
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Quietly' means with a tiny, soft sound — almost no sound at all."
  2. "You walk quietly when the baby is sleeping. You whisper quietly."
  3. "You can say 'shh, quietly' or 'play quietly.'"
