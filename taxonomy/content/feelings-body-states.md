# Feelings & Body States content sheet v1 — images + teaching descriptions

For review. Two pages: **Feelings** (emotions) and **How My Body Feels** (physical states).
Descriptions use: **what it means → the body signal (how you know) → when you feel it + what you can do/say.**
That middle "body signal" line is deliberate — it's how a child learns to *notice* the feeling, not just name it. The third line always offers a coping or communication move.
`{style}` = art style. `{reference}` = his likeness — every tile is him feeling it.

# FEELINGS  _(emotions)_

### happy
- **Image:** {reference} with a big joyful smile, bright eyes, arms up; warm sunny colors; caption "happy".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Happy' means you feel good and glad inside."
  2. "When you're happy, you smile, your eyes light up, and you might laugh or jump."
  3. "You might feel happy when you play or get a hug. You can say 'I'm happy!'"

### sad
- **Image:** {reference} with a downturned mouth, a tear on the cheek, slumped shoulders; soft blue tones; caption "sad".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Sad' means you feel down or unhappy inside."
  2. "When you're sad, your mouth turns down, your eyes get teary, and you may want a hug."
  3. "You might feel sad when you miss someone. You can say 'I'm sad' and ask for a hug."

### mad
- **Image:** {reference} with furrowed brows and crossed arms, a little puff of steam; caption "mad".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Mad' means you feel upset and angry, like something isn't fair."
  2. "Your face feels hot, your hands squeeze tight, and you might want to yell."
  3. "You might feel mad when a toy breaks. You can say 'I'm mad!' and take a big breath."

### scared
- **Image:** {reference} with wide eyes, hugging themselves, peeking at a soft shadow; gentle, not too spooky; caption "scared".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Scared' means you feel afraid that something might happen."
  2. "Your heart beats fast, your tummy flips, and you want to hide or hold someone."
  3. "You might feel scared of a loud noise or the dark. You can say 'I'm scared' and ask someone to stay with you."

### excited
- **Image:** {reference} with sparkling wide eyes, a huge grin, hands up mid-bounce, little stars; caption "excited".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Excited' means you feel super happy and can't wait for something."
  2. "Your heart beats fast and you might bounce, wiggle, and grin big."
  3. "You might feel excited about a party. You can say 'I'm so excited!'"

### silly
- **Image:** {reference} making a goofy crossed-eyes face with tongue out, giggling; caption "silly".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Silly' means you feel playful and goofy and want to be funny."
  2. "You make funny faces, giggle, and feel a little wild."
  3. "You might feel silly when you're joking around. You can say 'I'm being silly!'"

### surprised
- **Image:** {reference} with wide eyes, a round open mouth, hands on cheeks, "oh!"; caption "surprised".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Surprised' means something happened that you didn't expect."
  2. "Your eyes go wide, your mouth opens, and you might gasp."

### frustrated
- **Image:** {reference} struggling with a stuck puzzle piece, scrunched face, little fists; caption "frustrated".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Frustrated' means something is hard and it's making you upset."
  2. "Your body gets tense, you might groan, and you want to give up."
  3. "You might feel frustrated when a puzzle won't fit. You can say 'I'm frustrated — help me' and take a break."

### calm
- **Image:** {reference} sitting peacefully, soft eyes, taking a slow breath; gentle palette; caption "calm".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Calm' means you feel quiet, relaxed, and peaceful inside."

  3. "You might feel calm after a hug or a deep breath. You can say 'I feel calm.'"

### proud

### shy

### worried

### love

# HOW MY BODY FEELS  _(physical states)_

### hungry
- **Image:** {reference} pointing at their tummy with a hopeful look, a little rumble line; caption "hungry".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Hungry' means your tummy wants food."
  2. "Your tummy feels empty or makes a rumbly sound."
  3. "When you feel hungry, you can say 'I'm hungry' and ask for a snack."

### thirsty
- **Image:** {reference} reaching for a cup, touching their throat; caption "thirsty".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Thirsty' means your body wants a drink."
  2. "Your mouth and throat feel dry."
  3. "When you feel thirsty, you can say 'I'm thirsty' and ask for water."

### sleepy
- **Image:** {reference} yawning and rubbing one eye, droopy, little "zzz"; caption "sleepy".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Sleepy' means your body and eyes want to rest."
  2. "You yawn, rub your eyes, and feel slow and heavy."
  3. "When you feel sleepy, you can say 'I'm sleepy' and rest or take a nap."

### hurt
- **Image:** {reference} holding a knee with a little bandage, a bit teary; caption "hurt".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Hurt' means a part of your body feels owie or in pain."
  2. "It might sting, ache, or throb, and you might want to cry."
  3. "When something hurts, tell a grown-up where — like 'my knee hurts' — so they can help."

### my tummy hurts
- **Image:** {reference} holding their tummy with a slight frown; caption "my tummy hurts".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "This means your tummy feels sick or sore inside."
  2. "It might feel achy, crampy, or like you could throw up."
  3. "You can hold your tummy and say 'my tummy hurts' so a grown-up can help."

### my head hurts
- **Image:** {reference} touching their forehead with a small wince; caption "my head hurts".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "This means your head feels achy or sore."
  2. "It might feel tight or poundy, and bright light might bother you."
  3. "You can touch your head and say 'my head hurts' to tell a grown-up."

### sick
- **Image:** {reference} in bed with a thermometer, cheeks a little flushed, a tissue nearby; caption "sick".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Sick' means your body doesn't feel well all over."
  2. "You might feel hot, achy, sniffly, or tired."
  3. "When you feel sick, tell a grown-up 'I feel sick' so they can take care of you."

### itchy
- **Image:** {reference} scratching their arm by a little red spot; caption "itchy".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Itchy' means a spot on your skin makes you want to scratch."
  2. "It tickles and bothers you until you rub it."
  3. "When you feel itchy, you can say 'I'm itchy' and point to the spot."

### dizzy
- **Image:** {reference} a little wobbly with swirly stars circling their head; caption "dizzy".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Dizzy' means the room feels like it's spinning."
  2. "Your head feels wobbly and you might need to sit down."
  3. "When you feel dizzy, you can say 'I'm dizzy' and sit down to rest."

### need to potty
- **Image:** {reference} doing a little 'gotta-go' wiggle, pointing toward the bathroom; simple and tasteful; caption "potty".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "This means your body needs to use the toilet to pee or poop."
  2. "You feel a squeeze or a wiggle that says 'go now.'"
  3. "When you feel it, say 'I need to potty!' and head to the bathroom."

### full
- **Image:** {reference} gently pushing a plate away with a hand on a happy, full tummy; caption "full".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Full' means your tummy has had enough food — you're all done."
  2. "Your tummy feels satisfied and you don't want more."
  3. "When you're full, you can say 'all done' or 'I'm full.'"

### too hot
- **Image:** {reference} fanning their flushed face and tugging their collar; caption "too hot".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Too hot' means your body feels uncomfortably warm."
  2. "You get sweaty, your face flushes, and you want to cool down."
  3. "When you're too hot, you can say 'I'm too hot' and take off a layer or get a drink."

### too cold
- **Image:** {reference} shivering and hugging themselves, wanting a blanket; caption "too cold".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Too cold' means your body feels uncomfortably chilly."
  2. "You shiver, get goosebumps, and want to bundle up."
  3. "When you're too cold, you can say 'I'm too cold' and ask for a blanket or a hug."
