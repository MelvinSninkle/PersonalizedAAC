# Verb content sheet v1 — images + teaching descriptions

For review. Verb descriptions use three lenses: **what it means/does → how your body does it → when/where you'd use it** (with a phrase he can say where it fits).
`{style}` = art style token. `{reference}` = his likeness — verbs are embodiment-heavy, so he *sees himself doing it*.

---

## Requests  _(Ask someone to… / I want to…)_

### want
- **Image:** {reference} reaching both hands toward a toy just out of reach, hopeful eyes; caption "want".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Want' means you would really like to have or do something."
  2. "You can want a snack, want a toy, want to go, want a hug."
  3. "You can say 'I want that' or 'I want more.'"

### help
- **Image:** {reference} reaching up while a grown-up's hand reaches down to help open a tight jar; caption "help".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Help' means asking someone to do something with you or for you."
  2. "You ask for help to open a jar, reach a toy, or tie a shoe."
  3. "You can say 'help me!' or 'I need help.'"

### my turn
- **Image:** {reference} pointing happily at themselves while a friend holds out a toy; caption "my turn".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'My turn' means now it's your time to have a turn."
  2. "You take turns on the swing, with a toy, or in a game."
  3. "You can say 'my turn!' or 'is it my turn?'"

### again
- **Image:** {reference} clapping with delight, a little circular 'do-it-again' arrow above; caption "again".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Again' means do it one more time."
  2. "Sing it again, push me again, read it again."
  3. "You can say 'again!' or 'do it again.'"

## Move

### jump
- **Image:** {reference} caught mid-air, both feet off the ground, knees tucked, big grin, little motion lines under the feet; caption "jump".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you jump, you push off the ground with both feet and go up in the air."
  2. "Your knees bend down first, then spring you up high."
  3. "You can jump over a puddle, on the floor, or on a trampoline."

### run
- **Image:** {reference} running fast, one knee up, arms pumping, speed lines and a little dust; caption "run".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you run, you move your legs fast to go quickly."
  2. "Your arms pump and your feet barely touch the ground."
  3. "You run at the park, in a race, or to give someone a hug."

### climb
- **Image:** {reference} climbing a small playground ladder, reaching for the next rung, careful and proud; caption "climb".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you climb, you use your hands and feet to go up."
  2. "You hold on tight and pull yourself higher, step by step."
  3. "You climb a ladder, the stairs, or up to the slide."

### dance
- **Image:** {reference} dancing with arms up and a happy sway, music notes floating around; caption "dance".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you dance, you move your body to the music."
  2. "You wiggle, spin, clap, and bounce to the beat."
  3. "You can dance at a party, in your room, or when your song comes on."

### kick
- **Image:** {reference} kicking a soccer ball, leg swinging forward, the ball zooming away with motion lines; caption "kick".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you kick, you hit something with your foot to move it."
  2. "You swing your leg and your foot taps the ball away."
  3. "You kick a ball outside, or splash and kick in the water."

### fall
- **Image:** {reference} tumbling gently onto a soft cushion, surprised but okay; caption "fall".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Fall' means you tip over and go down to the ground."
  2. "Sometimes you trip, slip, or lose your balance and fall."
  3. "You can say 'I fell' or 'uh oh, I fell down.'"

## Everyday

### eat
- **Image:** {reference} taking a happy bite of a sandwich, a plate of food in front; caption "eat".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you eat, you put food in your mouth, chew it, and swallow."
  2. "You use your teeth to chew and your tongue to taste."
  3. "You eat at breakfast, lunch, and dinner when your tummy is hungry."

### drink
- **Image:** {reference} drinking from a cup with both hands, head tipped back a little; caption "drink".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you drink, you sip a liquid and swallow it."
  2. "You use a cup, a straw, or a bottle to drink."
  3. "You drink water when you're thirsty, or milk with a snack."

### sleep
- **Image:** {reference} curled up in bed with a teddy, eyes closed, little "zzz", a moon out the window; caption "sleep".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you sleep, you close your eyes and rest your whole body."
  2. "You lie down, get cozy, and your body rests until morning."
  3. "You sleep at night and sometimes take a nap in the day."

### wash hands
- **Image:** {reference} at a sink rubbing soapy, bubbly hands together, water running; caption "wash hands".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you wash your hands, you use soap and water to make them clean."
  2. "You rub the soap into bubbles, then rinse the bubbles away."
  3. "You wash your hands before you eat and after you use the potty."

### brush teeth
- **Image:** {reference} brushing teeth with a foamy smile, toothbrush and toothpaste; caption "brush teeth".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you brush, you clean your teeth with a toothbrush."
  2. "You put on toothpaste and brush up and down, all around."
  3. "You brush your teeth in the morning and before bed."

### potty
- **Image:** {reference} pointing toward a little toilet with a gentle 'I need to go' gesture; simple and tasteful; caption "potty".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Potty' means you need to use the toilet to pee or poop."
  2. "When your body gives you the signal, you go to the bathroom."
  3. "You can say 'I need to potty' or 'potty, please!'"

## Play & Make

### play

- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you play, you have fun with toys, games, or friends."
  2. "You can build, pretend, race, and laugh while you play."

### build
- **Image:** {reference} stacking colorful blocks into a tower, concentrating, proud; caption "build".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you build, you put pieces together to make something."
  2. "You stack, connect, and balance the pieces one at a time."

### draw

- **Descriptions:**

### read

- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you read, you look at the words and pictures in a book."
  2. "You turn the pages and follow the story one part at a time."
  3. "You can read at bedtime, or ask someone to read with you."

### sing
- **Image:** {reference} singing with mouth wide and happy, music notes floating up; caption "sing".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you sing, you make music with your voice."
  2. "You use your voice to go high and low with the tune."
  3. "You can sing a favorite song, or sing along in the car."

### throw
- **Image:** {reference} throwing a ball forward, arm extended, the ball leaving the hand with motion lines; caption "throw".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you throw, you send something flying through the air with your arm."

  3. "You can throw a ball to a friend, or a beanbag in a game."

## Hands

### open
- **Image:** {reference} lifting a box lid and peeking inside with curiosity; caption "open".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Open' means to undo something so it isn't closed anymore."
  2. "You lift, pull, or twist to open a box, a door, or a jar."
  3. "You can say 'open it' or 'help me open.'"

### push
- **Image:** {reference} pushing a toy shopping cart forward, leaning into it; caption "push".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you push, you press something to move it away from you. The opposite is pull."
  2. "You put your hands on it and press forward."
  3. "You push a cart, a swing, or a toy car."

### pull
- **Image:** {reference} pulling a wagon by its handle, leaning back; caption "pull".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you pull, you tug something to move it toward you. The opposite is push."
  2. "You grab it and lean back to bring it closer."
  3. "You pull a wagon, a rope, or open a drawer."

### give
- **Image:** {reference} holding out a flower with both hands to give to someone; caption "give".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you give, you hand something to someone else."
  2. "You hold it out and let them take it."
  3. "You can say 'here you go' or 'I give it to you.'"

### pour
- **Image:** {reference} carefully tipping a small pitcher into a cup, a steady stream of juice; caption "pour".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you pour, you tip a container so the liquid flows out."
  2. "You tilt it slowly so it goes into the cup, not everywhere."
  3. "You pour water, juice, or cereal into a bowl."

### hold
- **Image:** {reference} gently holding a puppy with both arms; caption "hold".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "When you hold something, you keep it in your hands or arms."
  2. "You wrap your fingers or arms around it so it stays."
  3. "You can say 'I'll hold it' or 'hold my hand.'"

## Stop & Go

### go
- **Image:** {reference} stepping forward eagerly and pointing ahead, a bright 'go' feel; caption "go".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Go' means to start moving or leave for somewhere."
  2. "You go to the park, go outside, go fast, go now."
  3. "You can say 'let's go!' or 'I want to go.'"

### stop
- **Image:** {reference} holding up a flat 'stop' hand, a small red stop-sign feel; caption "stop".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Stop' means to quit moving or stop doing something. The opposite is go."
  2. "You stop when something is too much, or it's time to be done."
  3. "You can say 'stop!' or 'please stop.'"

### wait
- **Image:** {reference} sitting patiently with hands in lap, calmly watching a clock; caption "wait".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Wait' means to stay calm until it's time."
  2. "Sometimes you wait for your turn, wait for food, or wait in line."
  3. "You can say 'I'll wait' or 'wait for me!'"

### done
- **Image:** {reference} pushing an empty plate away with two open 'all done' hands; caption "done".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Done' means you are finished — no more right now."
  2. "You can be done eating, done playing, or done with a job."
  3. "You can say 'all done!' or 'I'm done.'"

### come
- **Image:** {reference} waving someone over with a friendly 'come here' gesture; caption "come".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Come' means you want someone to come to you."
  2. "You wave them over or call them to your spot."
  3. "You can say 'come here' or 'come with me.'"

### show
- **Image:** {reference} proudly holding up a drawing to show someone; caption "show".
- **Descriptions:**
  1. "'Show' means to hold something up so someone can see it."
  2. "You lift it up and point it toward them."
  3. "You can say 'look what I made!' or 'I want to show you.'"

- **Image:** {reference} peeking under a blanket, searching for a hidden toy, curious; caption "find".
